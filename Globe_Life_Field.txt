<p><b>Globe Life Field</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Retractable_roof" title="Retractable roof">retractable roof</a> <a href="/wiki/Stadium" title="Stadium">stadium</a> in <a href="/wiki/Arlington,_Texas" title="Arlington, Texas">Arlington, Texas</a>. It is the <a href="/wiki/Baseball_park" class="mw-redirect" title="Baseball park">home ballpark</a> of <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Baseball" title="Major League Baseball">Major League Baseball</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Texas_Rangers_(baseball)" title="Texas Rangers (baseball)">Texas Rangers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sportsday_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sportsday-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> It is located just south of <a href="/wiki/Choctaw_Stadium" title="Choctaw Stadium">Choctaw Stadium</a>, the Rangers' former home ballpark.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Globe_Life_and_Accident_Insurance_Company" title="Globe Life and Accident Insurance Company">Globe Life and Accident Insurance Company</a> owns the naming rights for the facility through 2048.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Globe_Life_Field&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Globe_Life_Field&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:New_Rangers_Ballpark_001.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/New_Rangers_Ballpark_001.jpg/220px-New_Rangers_Ballpark_001.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/New_Rangers_Ballpark_001.jpg/330px-New_Rangers_Ballpark_001.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/New_Rangers_Ballpark_001.jpg/440px-New_Rangers_Ballpark_001.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4032" data-file-height="3024" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:New_Rangers_Ballpark_001.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Construction site in 2017. <a href="/wiki/AT%26T_Stadium" title="AT&amp;T Stadium">AT&amp;T Stadium</a> is visible in the background.</div></div></div><p>On May 20, 2016, the Rangers announced that they would vacate <a href="/wiki/Globe_Life_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Globe Life Park">Globe Life Park</a>. The new stadium was to be constructed in a public/private partnership and have a <a href="/wiki/Retractable_roof" title="Retractable roof">retractable roof</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MLB.com_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MLB.com-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The ballpark was approved on the following Election Day.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/HKS,_Inc." title="HKS, Inc.">HKS, Inc.</a> was announced as the architect on January 5, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-Texas_Rangers_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Texas_Rangers-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 31, 2019, the Rangers announced that the playing surface of Globe Life Field would be carpeted with synthetic grass supplied by Shaw Sports Turf, making them one of only five major league teams to play their home games on artificial turf.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Rangers cited weather as the reason why attendance at Globe Life Park was lower than in other baseball stadiums in major metropolitan areas, as the area is prone to high temperatures and frequent rain. Therefore, the Rangers proposed that their new ballpark should be constructed with a roof. Unlike its predecessors, the new stadium's center field faces northeast rather than southeast.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A new shopping mall, a <a href="/wiki/Loews_Hotel" class="mw-redirect" title="Loews Hotel">Loews Hotel</a>, and a ballpark village are planned to go along with the new stadium. <a href="/wiki/Choctaw_Stadium" title="Choctaw Stadium">Choctaw Stadium</a> will then be renovated for football and soccer use.
</p><p>The plans to build the stadium generated a mixed reaction. The new stadium offers a more comfortable environment to watch baseball but extended existing taxes used to pay for AT&amp;T Stadium. According to <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dallas_Morning_News" title="The Dallas Morning News">The Dallas Morning News</a></i>, "The deal calls for the city to issue $500 million in bonds to help pay for the stadium. A half-cent of sales tax, 2% hotel occupancy tax and 5% car rental tax would pay off those bonds over an estimated 30 years. Voters also approved a ticket tax of up to 10% and parking tax of up to $3 at the new stadium. That money would be used for some of the Rangers' portion of the debt, which was criticized by the opposition campaign."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On December 14, 2019, a section of the roof caught fire while under construction.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Arlington_June_2020_5_(Globe_Life_Field).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Arlington_June_2020_5_%28Globe_Life_Field%29.jpg/220px-Arlington_June_2020_5_%28Globe_Life_Field%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Arlington_June_2020_5_%28Globe_Life_Field%29.jpg/330px-Arlington_June_2020_5_%28Globe_Life_Field%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Arlington_June_2020_5_%28Globe_Life_Field%29.jpg/440px-Arlington_June_2020_5_%28Globe_Life_Field%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6000" data-file-height="4000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Arlington_June_2020_5_(Globe_Life_Field).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Globe Life Field in June 2020</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Opening">Opening</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Globe_Life_Field&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Opening">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Globe Life Field was originally scheduled to open on March 23, 2020, but because of the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, the start of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Major_League_Baseball_season" title="2020 Major League Baseball season">2020 Major League Baseball season</a> was delayed for several months.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Globe Life Field opened for a high-school graduation on May 29, 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-FOX_4_News_Dallas–Fort_Worth_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOX_4_News_Dallas–Fort_Worth-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 24, 2020, the Rangers hosted their first regular-season game against the <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Rockies" title="Colorado Rockies">Colorado Rockies</a>, which they won 1-0. The Rangers played two exhibition games against the Rockies on July 21 and 22 at Globe Life Field. <a href="/wiki/Joey_Gallo" title="Joey Gallo">Joey Gallo</a> hit the first <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home run</a> at the stadium on July 26. <sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2020_MLB_postseason">2020 MLB postseason</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Globe_Life_Field&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2020 MLB postseason">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Because of the COVID-19 pandemic, Major League Baseball announced on September 15, 2020 that it would implement a playoff "bubble" starting with the second round of the playoffs. Globe Life Field and <a href="/wiki/Minute_Maid_Park" title="Minute Maid Park">Minute Maid Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a> shared the <a href="/wiki/2020_National_League_Division_Series" title="2020 National League Division Series">2020 National League Division Series</a> second-round playoff series, with one series in Houston and the other in Arlington. The <a href="/wiki/2020_National_League_Championship_Series" title="2020 National League Championship Series">2020 National League Championship Series</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2020_World_Series" title="2020 World Series">2020 World Series</a> were played exclusively at Globe Life Field.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> MLB allowed fans to attend games at Globe Life during the NLCS and World Series.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="National_Finals_Rodeo">National Finals Rodeo</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Globe_Life_Field&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: National Finals Rodeo">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Professional_Rodeo_Cowboys_Association" title="Professional Rodeo Cowboys Association">Professional Rodeo Cowboys Association</a> held the 2020 <a href="/wiki/National_Finals_Rodeo" title="National Finals Rodeo">National Finals Rodeo</a> (NFR) at Globe Life Field instead of its usual location in Las Vegas due to the COVID-19 pandemic and Nevada's state-mandated health restrictions.<sup id="cite_ref-NFR_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NFR-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="College_sports">College sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Globe_Life_Field&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: College sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Army_vs._Air_Force_football_2021_15_(opening_kickoff).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Army_vs._Air_Force_football_2021_15_%28opening_kickoff%29.jpg/220px-Army_vs._Air_Force_football_2021_15_%28opening_kickoff%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Army_vs._Air_Force_football_2021_15_%28opening_kickoff%29.jpg/330px-Army_vs._Air_Force_football_2021_15_%28opening_kickoff%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Army_vs._Air_Force_football_2021_15_%28opening_kickoff%29.jpg/440px-Army_vs._Air_Force_football_2021_15_%28opening_kickoff%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6000" data-file-height="4000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Army_vs._Air_Force_football_2021_15_(opening_kickoff).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Opening kickoff of the 2021 Army vs. Air Force football game at Globe Life Field</div></div></div>
<p>On November 6, 2021, the ballpark hosted a <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">college football</a> game for the first time when <a href="/wiki/United_States_Military_Academy" title="United States Military Academy">Army</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force_Academy" title="United States Air Force Academy">Air Force</a> played in the Lockheed Martin Commanders' Classic. The Black Knights bested the Falcons, 21–14, in overtime. The day prior to the football game, the ballpark also hosted its first-ever boxing matches, with the boxing teams of each academy squaring off; Air Force won 6 bouts to 4.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Globe Life Field's football configuration has the end zones at left field and first base. The 2022 edition is scheduled for November 5.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_events">Notable events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Globe_Life_Field&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Notable events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="MLB">MLB</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Globe_Life_Field&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: MLB">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>On October 27, 2020, the <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Dodgers" title="Los Angeles Dodgers">Los Angeles Dodgers</a> defeated the <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Rays" title="Tampa Bay Rays">Tampa Bay Rays</a> in game 6 of the 2020 World Series. All 6 games of the <a href="/wiki/2020_World_Series" title="2020 World Series">2020 World Series</a> were held at Globe Life Field, making it the first and so far only venue to host a World Series without its tenant playing in the Series.</li>
<li>On April 9, 2021, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Musgrove" title="Joe Musgrove">Joe Musgrove</a> of the <a href="/wiki/San_Diego_Padres" title="San Diego Padres">San Diego Padres</a> threw the first <a href="/wiki/No-hitter" title="No-hitter">no-hitter</a> in <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_San_Diego_Padres" title="History of the San Diego Padres">Padres history</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On May 19, 2021, <a href="/wiki/Corey_Kluber" title="Corey Kluber">Corey Kluber</a> of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> threw the 12th no-hitter in <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_New_York_Yankees" title="History of the New York Yankees">Yankees history</a>.</li>
<li>On October 4, 2022, <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Judge" title="Aaron Judge">Aaron Judge</a> of the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Yankees" title="New York Yankees">New York Yankees</a> hit his 62nd <a href="/wiki/Home_run" title="Home run">home run</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2022_MLB_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2022 MLB season">2022 regular season</a> off of Rangers pitcher <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Tinoco" title="Jesús Tinoco">Jesús Tinoco</a>, breaking <a href="/wiki/Roger_Maris" title="Roger Maris">Roger Maris</a>'s long-standing record for most home runs hit during regular season play for an <a href="/wiki/American_League" title="American League">American League</a> player.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Concerts">Concerts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Globe_Life_Field&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Concerts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>On July 24, 2021, <a href="/wiki/Green_Day" title="Green Day">Green Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fall_Out_Boy" title="Fall Out Boy">Fall Out Boy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Weezer" title="Weezer">Weezer</a> performed the first concert at Globe Life Field, as the opening date of the <a href="/wiki/Hella_Mega_Tour" title="Hella Mega Tour">Hella Mega Tour</a>.</li>
<li>On November 27, 2021, <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">Metallica</a> had a concert as a part of Triller Fight Club on their <a href="/wiki/Metallica_2021%E2%80%932022_Tour" class="mw-redirect" title="Metallica 2021–2022 Tour">2021–2022 Tour</a>.</li>
<li>On August 23, 2022, <a href="/wiki/Lady_Gaga" title="Lady Gaga">Lady Gaga</a> performed at the stadium as a part of <a href="/wiki/The_Chromatica_Ball" title="The Chromatica Ball">The Chromatica Ball</a>.</li>
<li>On September 18, 2022, <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Strokes" title="The Strokes">The Strokes</a> performed at the stadium as a part of their <a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers_2022_Global_Stadium_Tour" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers 2022 Global Stadium Tour">2022 Global Stadium Tour</a>.</li>
<li>On October 8, 2022, <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Wallen" title="Morgan Wallen">Morgan Wallen</a> performed at the stadium as a part of his Dangerous Tour. Not only was it the fastest sellout in the venue's history, but he also broke the venue's attendance record with 43,598 fans coming to see him.</li></ul>
